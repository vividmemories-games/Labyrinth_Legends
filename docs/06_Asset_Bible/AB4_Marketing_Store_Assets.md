## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

Players and prospective players encounter Labyrinth Legends **before installation** — through store listings, social imagery, press materials, trailers, and community updates. Those touchpoints create expectations that the installed experience must honor.

**AB4 defines the production specification for every public-facing brand communication asset** — how each communication system presents the game honestly, builds trust, and remains consistent across the project lifetime.

AB4 answers:

> **How do we produce public brand communication that amplifies reality — never exaggerates it?**

### 1.2 Authority and Scope

| AB4 owns | AB4 does not own |
|----------|------------------|
| Public communication **system** responsibilities and production specs | Marketing strategy, advertising plans, budgets |
| Audience and expectation framing per communication system | ASO optimization, keyword strategy, publishing schedules |
| Contained public asset **categories** (not inventories) | In-product UI, menus, HUD ([AB3](AB3_UI_Brand_Assets.md)) |
| Consistency rules across public touchpoints | In-maze world assets ([AB2](AB2_Game_Assets.md)) |
| Brand evolution and reuse strategy for public surfaces | LLDL color, type, motion meaning (extends only) |
| Lifecycle guidance for campaigns and community assets | Legal policies, revenue strategy, business planning |
| Store and campaign visual production boundaries | Export settings, naming syntax, AI prompts (AB5–AB6) |

### 1.3 Inheritance

```text
Vision.md     — why the game exists
    ↓
Gameplay.md   — how the game works
    ↓
LLDL.md       — how the game looks, feels, moves, and communicates visually
    ↓
AB0 / AB1     — production philosophy and universal standards
    ↓
AB2           — playable-world asset systems (in-maze)
    ↓
AB3           — interface and in-product brand experiences
    ↓
AB4 (this doc)— public brand communication systems
    ↓
AB5–AB6       — AI pipeline, lifecycle gates
```

#### Vision

[Vision](../00_Project/Vision.md) requires premium craft, player respect, and calm tension. Public communication must feel **serious, timeless, and honest** — never noisy, manipulative, or trend-chasing for short-term attention.

#### Gameplay

[Gameplay](../01_Game_Design/Gameplay.md) centers **planning before action** — Draw Your Fate. Public assets must communicate study, commitment, and consequence — not real-time twitch action, combat spectacle, or mechanics the game does not deliver.

#### LLDL

[WS1 — Visual Identity](../02_Design_System/LLDL/WS1_Visual_Identity_Artistic_Direction.md) and related workshops define Ancient Tech × Mythical Ruins. AB4 **extends** LLDL into public production briefs. AB4 does not restate visual philosophy or redefine tokens.

#### AB0 and AB1

All public communication asset production inherits [AB0](AB0_Asset_Philosophy_Production_Principles.md) discipline and [AB1](AB1_Production_Standards.md) governance — briefs, approval, libraries, traceability.

#### AB2 Boundary

[AB2](AB2_Game_Assets.md) owns **in-maze** visuals used as reference material. AB4 may **showcase** authentic gameplay moments in store screenshots and campaign key art — but must not invent in-maze content that misrepresents mechanics or readability.

#### AB3 Boundary

[AB3](AB3_UI_Brand_Assets.md) owns **in-product** brand — logo lockups, splash, loading, ceremonial framing inside the app. AB4 extends identity to **public** surfaces — store icons, listing imagery, social crops, press kits. **In-product brand is authoritative**; public variants derive from approved masters, never the reverse.

| Asset role | Authority |
|------------|-----------|
| Logo and wordmark masters | AB3 canonical kit — AB4 produces public lockup variants |
| App icon source master | AB3 defines in-product reference — AB4 produces platform store variants |
| Splash and loading | AB3 — not store marketing frames |
| Store screenshots and feature graphics | AB4 |
| Social and campaign key art | AB4 |

#### Asset Bible and Technical Documentation

`Asset_Bible.md` integrates all workshops. Store platform requirements and implementation belong in technical documentation — AB4 specifies **what** public assets must communicate, not platform upload procedures.

### 1.4 What AB4 Deliberately Does Not Own

AB4 excludes marketing strategy, advertising budgets, ASO guides, publishing roadmaps, social media calendars, in-product UI, playable-world asset systems, and technical implementation. If content describes HUD layout, menu hierarchy, or maze tile production — it belongs to AB3 or AB2, not AB4.

## 2. Universal Brand Communication Principles

Every communication system inherits these principles **once**. Individual sections apply them; they do not restate them.

### 2.1 Brand Identity

Public communication expresses **one civilization** — ancient temple puzzle adventure with disciplined craft. No parallel "marketing game" that contradicts the installed experience.

### 2.2 Recognition

Players and prospects should recognize Labyrinth Legends across store, social, and community surfaces through **consistent motifs** — not through louder graphics or unrelated trends.

### 2.3 Expectation Management

Every asset answers: *What kind of experience am I being invited into?* Public communication sets **accurate** expectations about pacing, interaction model, and tone.

### 2.4 Trust

Long-term trust outweighs short-term attention. Assets that promise action the game does not deliver, hide monetization posture dishonestly, or use misleading screenshots erode premium positioning.

### 2.5 Authenticity

Marketing **amplifies reality**. Showcase real gameplay states, real UI chrome, and real visual language — composed with craft, not fabricated with false mechanics.

### 2.6 Consistency

Public systems share **frame vocabulary**, color discipline, and typographic tone with LLDL and AB3 — extended for external formats, not reinvented.

### 2.7 Player Lifecycle

Communication supports prospects before install and players after — without treating community as a separate brand. Advocacy grows from delivered experience.

### 2.8 Long-Term Reputation

Assets are produced for **years of reuse and refresh** — not disposable campaign noise. Libraries and masters reduce drift across updates.

### 2.9 Communication Philosophy

Public communication is **calm and confident** — ceremonial, not shouty. It respects attention the way Vision demands inside the game.

### 2.10 System Thinking

Public production is organized as **communication systems** and **reusable families** — not one-off hero images per post. See [AB1 §5.7](AB1_Production_Standards.md#57-systems-over-one-offs).

### Communication System Validation

Before any system is complete:

1. Supports **one or more stages** of the public communication lifecycle
2. Has **one primary** communication responsibility
3. Does **not overlap** another system
4. Reinforces **trust**
5. **Extends** — never replaces — higher authorities
6. Remains **scalable** for future campaigns, platforms, and releases

## 3. Public Communication Lifecycle

All communication systems support a single lifecycle arc:

```text
Awareness
    ↓
Interest
    ↓
Store Discovery
    ↓
Evaluation
    ↓
Installation
    ↓
Community
    ↓
Long-Term Advocacy
```

| Lifecycle stage | Primary AB4 system owner |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Awareness → Interest | Brand Identity · Campaign Communication |
| Store Discovery → Evaluation | Discovery & Store Presence |
| Installation handoff | Discovery & Store Presence → AB3 Brand Experience |
| Post-install engagement | Community Communication |
| Updates, sequels, platforms | Brand Evolution · Campaign Communication |

Prospects may enter at any stage; production must preserve **continuity** when they move between touchpoints.

### Brand Communication System Template

Every communication system in §4–§8 follows this internal structure:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Why this system exists |

| **Contained Assets** | Representative asset categories — not exhaustive inventories |
| **Consistency Rules** | Alignment with other public systems and higher authorities |
| **Lifecycle & Evolution** | How the system scales over time without identity drift |

## 4. Brand Identity

**Primary responsibility:** Define and protect the public expression of core brand identity.

### Purpose

Brand Identity establishes the **canonical public face** of Labyrinth Legends — how the game is recognized, positioned, and trusted before any specific campaign or store listing is authored.

### Audience

| Audience | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Primary** | Prospective players evaluating fit at first glance |
| **Secondary** | Press, creators, partners, and team members needing brand alignment |

### Communication Goal

Achieve instant **recognition** and **credible positioning** as a premium ancient puzzle adventure — calm, crafted, and honest about Draw Your Fate.

### Audience Expectations

People should immediately understand:

- This is **Labyrinth Legends** — not a generic puzzle or maze clone
- The fantasy is **ancient temples and ruins**, not modern arcade or cartoon chaos
- The experience is **thoughtful planning**, not reflex action
- Craft level signals **premium indie**, not asset-flip or trend-chasing

### Brand Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | One civilization — Ancient Tech × Mythical Ruins per LLDL |
| **Trust** | Positioning claims match Vision pillars and Gameplay truth |
| **Recognition** | Logo, icon, and key motifs repeat across public surfaces |
| **Authenticity** | Brand promise reflects installed experience |
| **Consistency** | Public lockups derive from AB3 masters — no rogue rebrands |

### Contained Assets

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Public logo lockups** | Horizontal, stacked, and compact marks for external use |
| **Wordmark treatments** | Ceremonial title presentation for campaigns and press |
| **Brand motif library** | Recurring symbols, frames, and ornamental elements |
| **Color and type application guides** | Production roles for public layouts — extends WS2/WS7, does not redefine |
| **Brand positioning statements** | Short approved phrases for external copy alignment — not ad strategy |
| **Public perception references** | Mood boards and do/don't examples for vendors |

### Consistency Rules

- All public logo variants **derive from** [AB3 Brand Experience](AB3_UI_Brand_Assets.md#4-brand-experience) canonical kit
- Gold remains **primary action and prestige** in public layouts; cyan remains **path and energy** — per LLDL discipline
- No cyberpunk neon, cartoon exaggeration, or generic mobile-game gradients
- Brand Identity assets are **versioned** — changes require explicit approval and AB3 alignment check

### Lifecycle & Evolution

- Brand Identity updates **rarely** — major version bumps only with Human approval
- New platforms adopt existing masters before inventing alternate marks
- Franchise growth extends **motif vocabulary** — not new visual dialects
- AI-generated brand exploration converges on approved lockups — human review mandatory (AB5 extends)

## 5. Discovery & Store Presence

**Primary responsibility:** Convert store discovery into confident installation through honest first impressions.

### Purpose

Discovery & Store Presence governs every asset a prospect sees **on platform storefronts** — the critical bridge between public interest and AB3's in-product brand experience.

### Audience

| Audience | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Primary** | Store visitors comparing puzzle adventures |
| **Secondary** | Platform editorial teams, featuring reviewers, search-driven browsers |

### Communication Goal

Communicate **what the game is**, **how it plays**, and **why it feels premium** — clearly enough that installation leads to satisfied expectations, not surprise.

### Audience Expectations

Store visitors should understand:

- Core loop: **study maze → draw path → commit → watch execution**
- Tone: calm temple exploration with fair puzzle challenge
- Visual quality matches screenshots **after install**
- No hidden bait-and-switch mechanics (joystick action, combat, endless runners)

### Brand Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Store frames feel like temple interface — not stock app templates |
| **Trust** | Screenshots show **authentic** gameplay states and real UI |
| **Recognition** | Icon and feature graphics match brand motif library |
| **Authenticity** | No staged scenes with mechanics that do not exist |
| **Consistency** | iOS and Android listings share one visual story — platform-safe crops only |

### Contained Assets

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **App icon — store variants** | Platform-optimized crops from AB3 icon master |
| **Store screenshots** | Composed gameplay and meta-game moments — truthful states |
| **Feature graphics / headers** | Store banner and promotional header treatments |
| **Store preview videos** | Short loops showing planning and execution — calm pacing |
| **Store copy alignment visuals** | Typography and frame treatments supporting listing text |
| **Platform badge and rating frames** | Optional ceremonial framing for accolades — not deceptive |

Store copy **wording** belongs to product/marketing planning — AB4 governs the **visual assets** that accompany listings.

### Consistency Rules

- Screenshot composition uses **approved in-maze and UI states** — AB2 and AB3 references required
- First screenshot answers *what kind of game is this?* — typically planning or maze study moment
- Feature graphics reuse **Brand Identity** motifs — no one-off campaign art as permanent store header
- App icon store variants **must remain recognizable** against AB3 master at small sizes
- Accessibility: key screenshot text legible at thumbnail scale; sufficient contrast on store frames

### Lifecycle & Evolution

- Store sets refresh for **major releases** — not weekly churn
- New worlds or features add screenshots to **existing narrative sequence** — study, plan, commit, reward
- Platform specification changes trigger **crop review** — not full rebrand
- A/B visual tests, if used, must stay within approved asset families — strategy is out of scope; honesty is not

## 6. Campaign Communication

**Primary responsibility:** Announce moments, updates, and events with brand discipline.

### Purpose

Campaign Communication covers **time-bound public pushes** — launch, content updates, seasonal moments, and promotional key art — without inventing a separate visual language per campaign.

### Audience

| Audience | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Primary** | Interested prospects and installed players considering return |
| **Secondary** | Social followers, ad networks, press during launch windows |

### Communication Goal

Create **focused attention** for a specific moment while preserving long-term brand recognition and truthful gameplay positioning.

### Audience Expectations

Audiences should understand:

- **What** is being announced (launch, update, event)
- **Why** it matters to the puzzle adventure experience
- **When** it is available — without false urgency
- Visuals reflect **real** game content for the announced moment

### Brand Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Campaign art extends motif library — not new genre aesthetics |
| **Trust** | Launch art shows representative gameplay — not cinematic fiction |
| **Recognition** | Campaign templates reuse brand frames and typography roles |
| **Authenticity** | Seasonal events use in-world visual language — AB2/AB3 aligned |
| **Consistency** | Campaign systems plug into Brand Identity — not parallel art direction |

### Contained Assets

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Launch key art** | Primary announcement hero — honest premium positioning |
| **Update announcement art** | New content, worlds, or feature reveals |
| **Seasonal / event key art** | Time-bound ceremonial treatments |
| **Social crop templates** | Aspect-ratio-safe frames for platforms |
| **Paid media asset families** | Variants within approved campaign template — not open-ended styles |
| **Promotional motion loops** | Short branded animations for ads and social — calm motion discipline |

### Consistency Rules

- Every campaign begins from **approved brand template** — custom one-offs require Human approval
- Key art may dramatize composition but **not mechanics** — no fake joystick UI, combat, or false HUD
- Update campaigns reference **shippable content** — not roadmap speculation
- Campaign color and type extend LLDL — campaign saturation does not override WS2 roles
- After campaign ends, reusable assets return to **library** — disposable art is discouraged

### Lifecycle & Evolution

- **Launch kit** is produced once, refreshed at major milestones — not rebuilt per post
- Campaign templates accumulate as **versioned families** — AB5 may assist variation within family
- Retired campaigns archive assets for reference — do not delete brand history silently
- Cross-platform campaigns share **one master composition** — crops derive, not recompose from scratch

## 7. Community Communication

**Primary responsibility:** Maintain trust with installed players and public observers through ongoing communication.

### Purpose

Community Communication governs **ongoing public dialogue assets** — patch notes presentation, developer updates, announcements, press materials, and creator-facing kits — reinforcing that the studio respects players and delivers honestly.

### Audience

| Audience | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Primary** | Installed players seeking clarity on changes and direction |
| **Secondary** | Press, creators, forum communities, and prospective players observing studio behavior |

### Communication Goal

Sustain **long-term trust** through clear, calm, consistent communication — turning satisfied players into advocates without hype manipulation.

### Audience Expectations

Communities should understand:

- **What changed** and **why** — especially balance and fairness ([GP4](../01_Game_Design/Gameplay/GP4_Hazards_Failure.md) alignment in tone, not rules)
- Studio voice is **respectful and direct** — not condescending or overly casual meme brand
- Visual presentation matches game identity — community graphics are not a separate aesthetic
- Creator and press materials are **accurate** and **easy to use**

### Brand Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Community templates use ceremonial framing — same temple instrument |
| **Trust** | Patch and update visuals do not oversell minor fixes |
| **Recognition** | Recurring header and thumbnail templates build familiarity |
| **Authenticity** | Developer updates show real work — concept art labeled as such |
| **Consistency** | Press kit assets match store and campaign identity |

### Contained Assets

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Patch notes header templates** | Consistent framing for update posts |
| **Developer update banners** | Roadmap and behind-the-scenes communication visuals |
| **Community announcement cards** | Events, maintenance, and important notices |
| **Press kit asset pack** | Logo, screenshots, key art, fact sheet layout frames |
| **Creator communication templates** | Stream overlays, thumbnail frames — within brand bounds |
| **FAQ and support article headers** | Public help communication framing |

Legal policy text and moderation rules belong elsewhere — AB4 covers **visual production** for community surfaces.

### Consistency Rules

- Community graphics **never** use alarm colors or aggressive urgency unless genuine critical outage
- Press kits include **approved** screenshots only — from Discovery & Store Presence library
- Creator templates forbid **misleading** gameplay overlays — no fake UI or exaggerated effects
- Patch note visuals scale for **localization** — frames tolerate text expansion
- Community voice visuals align with AB3 System Experience tone — calm utility, not marketing shout

### Lifecycle & Evolution

- Community templates version with **product seasons** — incremental refresh, not annual rebrand
- Press kit updates when **store set** or **major content** changes — single source sync
- Archive prior press assets when superseded — maintain changelog for media partners
- Advocacy programs reuse **Brand Identity** motifs — loyalty graphics do not invent new mascots without approval

## 8. Brand Evolution

**Primary responsibility:** Preserve identity while scaling across time, platforms, and franchise growth.

### Purpose

Brand Evolution defines how public communication assets **age, extend, and multiply** without diluting recognition — the long-horizon system for franchise-scale brand stewardship.

### Audience

| Audience | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Primary** | Internal leadership and brand guardians planning multi-year releases |
| **Secondary** | Partners evaluating franchise potential; returning players measuring continuity |

### Communication Goal

Ensure Labyrinth Legends remains **recognizable and trusted** across sequels, platforms, spin-offs, and years of operation — without freezing creativity or encouraging identity drift.

### Audience Expectations

Stakeholders should understand:

- Future releases **belong to the same civilization**
- Visual refresh **deepens** craft — does not pivot genre
- Legacy assets remain **honorable** — old campaigns do not contradict current identity
- Platform expansion does not mean **brand fragmentation**

### Brand Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Evolution extends WS1/AB3 foundations — not replacement |
| **Trust** | Major identity shifts require Human approval and player communication |
| **Recognition** | Motif continuity across years — logo evolution is rare and deliberate |
| **Authenticity** | New platforms showcase real builds — not speculative concept |
| **Consistency** | Reuse libraries before commissioning net-new public art |

### Contained Assets

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Brand version archive** | Historical lockups and campaign masters — reference only |
| **Platform extension kits** | Console, PC, or web store adaptations — when in scope |
| **Franchise extension guidelines** | How spin-offs or sequels inherit public identity |
| **Refresh cadence documentation** | When store sets and campaign templates may update |
| **Deprecation notices** | Marking retired public assets — prevents accidental reuse |
| **Recognition audit checklist** | Periodic review that public surfaces still match AB3/LLDL |

### Consistency Rules

- **Reuse before reinvent** — per AB0 lifecycle philosophy
- New platforms adopt **existing** screenshot and icon families before custom art
- Franchise growth adds **world motifs** — not new genre positioning
- Brand Evolution coordinates with AB3 when in-product brand updates — **single coordinated release**
- AB5/AB6 govern how evolution assets are produced and gated — AB4 defines **what must stay true**

### Lifecycle & Evolution

- Annual **recognition audit** recommended — not mandatory rebrand
- Major evolution (logo, icon, core motif) requires: AB3 sync, store refresh plan, press kit update, Human lock
- Deprecated assets remain in archive — never silently deleted
- Long-term advocacy depends on **delivered experience** — Brand Evolution cannot compensate for product drift

## 9. Conclusion

### 9.1 How Systems Work Together

Public communication production is one **continuous brand instrument**:

```text
Brand Identity (recognize)
        ↓
Discovery & Store Presence (evaluate → install)
        ↓
Campaign Communication (announce moments)
        ↓
Community Communication (sustain trust)
        ↓
Brand Evolution (preserve across time)
        ↓
Handoff to AB3 in-product experience
```

Each system owns one primary communication responsibility. Overlap creates confused positioning and production debt.

### 9.2 Inheritance Summary

| Authority | AB4 inherits by… |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Vision** | Honest premium positioning; respect for player attention |
| **Gameplay** | Showcasing planning, commitment, and consequence — never false mechanics |
| **LLDL / WS1** | Producing public assets that express visual identity — not redefining it |
| **AB0 / AB1** | Governance, libraries, approval on all public communication assets |
| **AB2** | Using authentic in-maze reference — not inventing misleading world content |
| **AB3** | Deriving public brand from in-product canonical kit |

### 9.3 Foundation for AB5 and AB6

| Workshop | Relationship to AB4 |
|----------|---------------------|
| **AB5** | AI pipeline for public communication asset families defined here |
| **AB6** | Lifecycle gates apply AB1 state model to AB4 deliverables |

## 10. Governance

### 10.1 Document Authority

AB4 is subordinate to Vision, Gameplay, LLDL, AB0, AB1, AB2, and AB3 (for in-product brand boundary). AB4 is authoritative for public brand communication production specifications.

### 10.2 Document Lifecycle

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Draft** | Active authorship |
| **Approved** | Reviewer acceptance |
| **Approved — Locked** | Human-locked; version bump for changes |

AB4 should normally be approved and locked after AB3 to preserve Asset Bible dependency order. Exceptions require explicit Human approval.

### 10.3 Review Methodology

Follow LLDS compiler workflow — review package, Codex, ChatGPT, Human approval. See [99_Reviews](../99_Reviews/README.md).

## 11. What AB4 Must Not Do

AB4 must **not**:

- Define marketing strategy, advertising plans, budgets, or ASO optimization
- Define publishing schedules, revenue strategy, or business planning
- Specify gameplay mechanics, puzzle logic, or movement rules
- Govern in-maze world assets (AB2) or in-product UI (AB3)
- Redefine LLDL color, typography, motion, or visual identity meaning
- Create wireframes, Figma files, Flutter implementation, or platform upload procedures
- Define legal policies, moderation rules, or QA processes
- Specify export formats, naming syntax, or AI prompts (AB5–AB6)
- Duplicate AB0, AB1, AB3, or WS1 prose — reference and extend
